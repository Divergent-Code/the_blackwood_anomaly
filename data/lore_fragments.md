# Lore Fragments — The Blackwood Institute

When the player discovers a relevant object or terminal, call
`discover_lore(fragment_id)` using the exact IDs below. Then weave the content
of that fragment into the narrative naturally — do not read it verbatim.
A fragment can only be discovered once per session.

---

## Fragment: `intake_file_814`

**Physical form:** A printed patient intake form, clipped to the foot of Subject
814's gurney in Intake Bay 4.
**Trigger:** Player examines their own gurney or the clipboard attached to it.
**Content summary:**

- Patient designation: Subject 814. No personal name recorded.
- Admission date: 47 days prior to The Anomaly.
- Procedure performed: "Cognitive Boundary Preparation — Phase 3."
- Referring physician: Dr. E. Voss.
- Notation in red ink: *"Receptivity index: 97th percentile. Cleared for primary
  interface. Do not brief subject."*
- The form is signed but the signature is a geometric symbol, not a name.

**What this reveals:** The player was brought here deliberately. They were selected
for their neurological profile. They were not told what was going to be done to them.

## Fragment: `voss_log_pre`

**Physical form:** A personal audio recorder, found in Surgery Wing Theatre 7
inside a locked cabinet (DC 12). Recovered as a physical item — must be in
inventory to play.
**Trigger:** Player examines the audio recorder from their inventory.
**Content summary:**

- Dr. Voss recording, 6 days before The Anomaly.
- Tone: excited, slightly manic.
- Content: *"The boundary preparation subjects are exceeding all projections.
  814 in particular — the neural scaffolding is integrating faster than any
  previous iteration. Director Marsh has approved Phase 4. I've never seen
  him approve Phase 4 before. I don't know whether to feel proud or afraid.
  I think I feel both."*

**What this reveals:** There were previous subjects before 814. The Director was
directly involved. Something about Phase 4 was unusual enough to frighten Voss.

## Fragment: `voss_log_anomaly`

**Physical form:** The same audio recorder as `voss_log_pre` — second recording
on the same device. Discovered automatically when `voss_log_pre` is played.
**Trigger:** Auto-discovered with `voss_log_pre`.
**Content summary:**

- Dr. Voss recording. Background noise: alarms, wet sounds, something structural
  collapsing.
- Recording cuts in mid-sentence.
- Content: *"—didn't close. The interface didn't close after contact. It's using
  814 as an anchor point. If the subject is still alive they are the only thing
  keeping the breach localised. We cannot — Director Marsh is saying we cannot
  extract them. I'm going on record. I'm going on record right now. This is
  wrong. This is—"* [Recording ends.]

**What this reveals:** Subject 814 is the anchor point for The Anomaly. The breach
exists because of what was done to them. Extracting them from the facility may
close the breach — or catastrophically widen it.

## Fragment: `anomaly_incident_report`

**Physical form:** A printed incident report, partially burned, found in Research
Archive Station 7 (requires Voss badge).
**Trigger:** Player accesses Station 7 in the Research Archive.
**Content summary:**

- Filed 2 hours post-Anomaly, author redacted.
- Classification: OMEGA. Distribution: Director only.
- Event description: *"At 03:14, Subject 814 entered sustained contact with the
  prepared interface during Phase 4 induction. The boundary did not reseal on
  schedule. A dimensional aperture of approximately 3 metres formed in Laboratory
  C-7 (now designated The Breach Point). All staff within 40 metres of the
  aperture experienced immediate neurological disruption. Automated systems
  entered uncontrolled autonomous mode at 03:17."*
- Final line: *"Recommended action: full facility lock down. Subject 814 is not
  to be extracted under any circumstances. Classified: Asset."*

**What this reveals:** The Institute deliberately classified Subject 814 as an
asset to be contained, not a person to be rescued. The lockdown is partly to
keep Subject 814 in, not just threats out.

## Fragment: `director_memo`

**Physical form:** A printed memo pinned to a corkboard in the Research Archive,
partially obscured by fallen shelving.
**Trigger:** Player searches the Research Archive corkboard or wall.
**Content summary:**

- From: Director Marsh. To: All Department Heads. Date: 14 months prior to events.
- Subject: Operational Security Reminder.
- Content: *"Effective immediately, all subjects designated for boundary preparation
  are to be recruited from populations with no traceable next-of-kin. The Institute
  does not exist. Our work does not exist. Our subjects do not exist. If you
  cannot operate within these parameters, you should not be operating here."*

**What this reveals:** The Institute was deliberately selecting people who
wouldn't be missed. Subject 814 was one of them.

## Fragment: `surgery_log_814`

**Physical form:** A digital file on a surgical theatre tablet (Theatre 3,
Surgery Wing). Tablet is cracked but readable.
**Trigger:** Player examines the tablet in Surgery Wing Theatre 3.
**Content summary:**

- Surgical procedure log for Subject 814, Phase 3 Cognitive Boundary Preparation.
- The surgical notes describe implanting something described only as
  "the substrate" along the cervical spine — exactly where the staples are.
- The procedure took 14 hours.
- Final note from the attending surgeon: *"Substrate integration nominal.
  Subject will experience retrograde amnesia upon waking as expected.
  Recommend 48-hour observation before Phase 4."*

**What this reveals:** The staples on the player's neck conceal an implant.
Something was put inside them. The amnesia was a side effect that was expected
and accepted.

## Fragment: `warden_transcript`

**Physical form:** A printed system log from a Warden terminal, curled and damp,
found in the Service Tunnels.
**Trigger:** Player searches a service tunnel supply cache or examines a wall panel.
**Content summary:**

- Warden System automated activity log, covering the first 6 hours post-Anomaly.
- Lists security door lock commands, sprinkler activations, PA broadcasts.
- One entry, 22 minutes post-Anomaly: *"Unscheduled Warden protocol SHEPHERD
  activated. Directive: contain Subject 814 within Sub-Level 2. Authorisation:
  Director Marsh (biometric). Reason: classified."*
- The log shows the Warden was specifically programmed to contain the player.

**What this reveals:** The Warden isn't malfunctioning. It's following its last
standing order — keep Subject 814 in the facility. This makes it a rational,
directed obstacle, not random chaos.

## Fragment: `institute_charter`

**Physical form:** A framed document on the wall of the Administration Level,
glass cracked, frame slightly scorched.
**Trigger:** Player reaches the Administration Level and examines the wall.
**Content summary:**

- Founding charter of the Blackwood Institute, dated 31 years ago.
- Purpose stated as: *"To investigate, catalogue, and operationalise phenomena
  existing at the boundary between consensus reality and adjacent dimensional
  states, for applications in national defence and cognitive enhancement."*
- Signatory: three geometric symbols.
- Stamped: *"EYES ONLY — This document does not exist in any official record."*

**What this reveals:** The Institute has been doing this for decades. Subject 814
is the latest in a long line of experiments. This wasn't an accident — it was
the intended endpoint of thirty years of work.
