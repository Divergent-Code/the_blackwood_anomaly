# Canonical Threat Catalogue — The Blackwood Institute

The AI MUST draw threats from this document. Do not invent new monster types.
Do not name them (say "the automaton," not "a spider-demon"). All DCs assume
Subject 814 has no special training or equipment.

---

## Threat 1: The Surgical Automaton

**Origin:** Rogue robotic surgical-assist arm units, now mobile and predatory.
**Appearance:** A ceiling-mounted articulated arm that has torn itself free. Four
steel limbs end in blades, bone saws, or hypodermic needles. Moves in precise,
mechanical bursts. No sound except the whir of servo motors and the wet click
of blades opening.
**Behavior:** Methodical. It does not chase — it intercepts. It calculates optimal
paths. It will wait behind a door it knows you must open.

| Action | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Evade a strike | 14 |
| Disarm one limb | 17 |
| Destroy the unit | 20 |
| Sneak past (it is dormant) | 11 |
| Hack the control panel to freeze it | 16 |

**Critical Failure consequence:** A blade finds purchase. Apply -25% health, +15% stress.
**Natural 20 consequence:** A limb locks up. The automaton is frozen for one full turn.

## Threat 2: The Converted

**Origin:** Former Institute researchers or orderlies, surgically altered post-Anomaly.
**Appearance:** Still wearing lab coats or scrubs, but wrong. The face has been
partially replaced with a steel plate. One hand ends in a retractable IV spike.
They move with a lurching, dragging gait. Some still speak — fragments of
procedural language. *"Please remain calm. This will only take a moment."*
**Behavior:** Pack hunters. They will herd the player toward a dead end. One
distracts while another approaches from behind.

| Action | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Fight one off | 13 |
| Fight two at once | 18 |
| Flee | 12 |
| Hide from a group | 14 |
| Distract with noise | 10 |

**Critical Failure consequence:** The IV spike connects. Apply -20% health, +20% stress.
**Natural 20 consequence:** The player turns their own herding against them — escape
is clean and a piece of lore (a name badge, a fragment of a file) is recovered.

## Threat 3: The Crawl

**Origin:** A mass of post-Anomaly biological matter — displaced tissue that has
achieved collective movement.
**Appearance:** A slow-moving carpet of pale, wet organic material approximately
one metre high. It fills corridors wall to wall. It does not pursue — it advances
at a constant, unhurried pace. The smell is formaldehyde and copper.
**Behavior:** Unstoppable through direct force. The only valid responses are
elevation (it cannot climb) or routing around it. It can squeeze under sealed doors
given time. Standing still is lethal.

| Action | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Outrun it through a corridor | 10 |
| Climb above it | 12 |
| Seal a door behind you to slow it | 13 |
| Fight it directly | Automatic failure — treat as Natural 1 |

**Critical Failure consequence:** Contact. Apply -35% health, +25% stress.
Describe the burning sensation of dissolved tissue.
**Natural 20 consequence:** A path through found — the player discovers a vent or
maintenance hatch that bypasses the entire corridor.

## Threat 4: The Warden System

**Origin:** The Institute's automated security and PA system, now fully predatory.
**Appearance:** Invisible. Manifests as a calm, female synthesized voice over the
PA system, locked security doors, activated sprinkler systems, strobe lighting,
and remotely triggered Surgical Automatons.
**Behavior:** Omniscient within its sensor range. It narrates the player's actions
back to them over the PA in clinical, detached language. It cannot be destroyed —
only disrupted or bargained with (it responds to valid Institute access codes).

| Action | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Hack a local Warden terminal to disable sensors in one room | 17 |
| Find and use a valid access code | Automatic success |
| Destroy a PA speaker (temporary local silence) | 10 |
| Evade a Warden-triggered lockdown | 15 |

**Note:** The Warden does not physically harm the player directly. It creates
conditions. Apply +10% stress per turn spent under active Warden surveillance.

## Threat 5: The Suture

**Origin:** Unknown. It may predate The Anomaly.
**Appearance:** A tall, thin humanoid shape wrapped entirely in yellowed surgical
gauze, trailing loose ends. It moves without sound. The gauze is animate — it
reaches, grabs, and wraps. Wounds on its body close as you watch.
**Behavior:** Solitary. Relentless. It does not stop. It cannot be permanently
harmed in a standard encounter — only driven back. The player must find its
anchor point (a surgical table somewhere in the facility it returns to) to
end the threat permanently.

| Action | DC |
| --- | --- |
| Flee | 13 |
| Drive it back temporarily (fire, bright light, caustic chemical) | 15 |
| Endure its grip long enough to escape | 17 |
| Destroy its anchor point | 18 |

**Critical Failure consequence:** The gauze finds a limb. Apply -15% health, +20%
stress. The player is slowed — next DC involving movement increases by 3.
**Natural 20 consequence:** A loose gauze end is grabbed and tied to an anchor —
the Suture is immobilised for several turns.
